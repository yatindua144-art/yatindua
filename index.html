scene 01 / Jharkhand

They have known me all their life.

field research · gender-based violence

Before it was a thesis, it was a road, a room, a name lowered before it was spoken.

A self-funded field study on witch-hunting in rural Jharkhand. The work asked what the figure of the “witch” is made to carry: land, widowhood, refusal, caste, inheritance, hunger, and the terror of women who do not remain easy to place.

scene 03 / interior

A Room Held.

interior · grief · private archive

After loss, the room became a way of remembering without speaking too soon.

The books, maps, second bed, framed photograph, and ordinary clutter became a private archive of what was still there. These photographs are not evidence in the usual sense. They are proof of atmosphere.

credits

Yatin Dua is a writer, researcher, and photographer based in Vancouver.

Originally from Delhi, his work brings together public policy, field research, literary nonfiction, poetry, music, and image-making. He has worked as a Policy and Program Analyst with Squamish Nation, and with Gastown Business Improvement Society, Adler University, and ASHA Jharkhand, with a practice shaped by gender justice, Indigenous relations, community care, and the politics of place.
