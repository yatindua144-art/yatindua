My work sits between language and analysis, between lived experience and public form. I am interested in how memory, politics, identity, violence, place, and desire become legible through writing, sound, and image.

§ 03 — Poetry

Poetry

Selected poems and lyric fragments. A register that carries what the essay cannot — the image that refuses to argue, the sentence that returns. Often illustrated; often in conversation with Fanon, Spivak, Bourdieu.

It's burning and bleeding Dry
Saffron, white and Green
Hues of rainbow in between
Agenda fixed, program controlled
Italian, lotus and more Indeed
In the name of Saffron and green.

From Burning Dry · Gaysi Family · 2021 · Illustrations by Yogee Chandra
On the page

Illustrated poetry has been a way of letting the theoretical voice and the lyric voice share a margin. The epigraphs do not translate the poem; they stand beside it.

Forthcoming work returns to the same registers — memory, displacement, desire — with photographs replacing the illustrations.

§ 06 — Research

Research

Scholarly and policy writing with short plain-language abstracts. Three registers: field research, applied policy analysis, and contributed work for institutions. Full documents available on request.

  • 2019 – 2020 MA Thesis · TERI 112 interviews Self-funded

    Witch-hunting and gender-based violence in rural Jharkhand

    Plain-language abstract

    A field study of how the category of the witch is deployed in rural Jharkhand, and what it is made to carry: land disputes, widowhood, refusal. The thesis argues that any serious policy response has to begin with the political economy of the accusation itself, not the folk-belief framing that dominates state reports.

  • 2025 – 2026 Squamish Nation Program policy Applied

    Indigenous workforce development & the green transition

    Plain-language abstract

    Program analysis at the Squamish Nation Training and Trades Centre on the gap between trades enrolment projections and the labour requirements of the BC clean-economy pipeline. Findings informed the accompanying op-ed More than stewards.

  • 2023 Adler · Heiltsuk Community research Applied

    Wosseyoo — Indigenous maternal health application

    Plain-language abstract

    A distinctions-based maternal health tool built with and for the Heiltsuk Nation in Bella Bella. Research role as part of the Adler MPPA programme, 500 hours of applied fieldwork, trauma-informed practice throughout.

  • 2022 European Commission Contributor Published

    SWITCH-Asia — food consumption patterns

    Plain-language abstract

    Contributed analysis to the SWITCH-Asia programme's review of food consumption transitions, with emphasis on rural-urban divergence in South Asia.

§ 07 — About

About

A short biography. Longer CV and writing samples available on request; full list of publications and fellowships below.

Portrait · Vancouver · 2026

Yatin Dua is a writer and researcher working across poetry, essays, scholarship, music, and visual practice. His work brings together artistic, intellectual, and public-facing forms in one space.

Originally from Delhi, based in Vancouver. A Program Officer at the Squamish Nation Training and Trades Centre through 2026; previously fieldwork in rural Jharkhand on witch-hunting and gender-based violence, and a research role on the Wosseyoo Indigenous maternal health project with the Heiltsuk Nation. Selected as an Action Canada Fellow (2024) and as a WTTC Global Summit Youth Delegate (Riyadh, 2022).
